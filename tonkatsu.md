# Tonkatsu

## Ingredients
- 2 boneless pork loin chops or tenderloin fillets, ½ inch thick 
- 1 large egg
- 2/3 cup panko
- 2 tablespoon all-purpose flour 
- ½ tablespoon olive oil (for the egg)
- 1 tablespoon olive oil (if baking)
- Oil for frying 
- Salt
- Black pepper
- [Tonkatsu Sacue](tonkatsu-sauce.md)

## Preparation

### Baking
1. Optionally, warm a pan over medium heat and add the panko and 1 tablespoon of oil to a frying pan. Stir the panko periodically and toast until golden brown. 
2. Cut slits through the fat layer of the pork roughly every half inch. This will keep tonkatsu flat after cooking.
3. Pound both sides of the pork with a meat mallet to tenderize it. 
4. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
6. Crack an egg into a dish, add 1/2 tablespoon oil and whisk together until well combined. 
7. Dredge pork in flour and shake off excess. 
8. Dip pork into egg mixture and then dredge in panko. Gently press the panko into the pork and then shake off excess. 
10. Place the pork on a wire rack and bake at 400&deg;F until inside temperature registers 145&deg;F. About 20 minutes.

### Frying
1. Cut slits through the fat layer of the pork roughly every half inch. This will keep tonkatsu flat after cooking.
2. Pound both sides of the pork with a meat mallet to tenderize it. 
3. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
4. Crack an egg into a dish, add 1/2 tablespoon oil and whisk together until well combined. 
5. Dredge pork in flour and shake off excess. 
6. Dip pork into egg mixture and then dredge in panko. Gently press the panko into the pork and then shake off excess. 
7. Let the breaded pork sit for 5-10 minutes to allow the breading to set.
8. Heat oil to 340&deg;F;
9. Gently place one piece of pork into the oil and cook for 1 minute without touching the pork.
10. Flip the pork and cook on the other side for 1 minute.   
11. Remove pork from oil and place on wire rack or paper towel.
12. Remove any remaining bread crumbs from the oil with a fine-mesh strainer and allow oil to return to 240&deg;F.
13. Repeat 9-12 with remaining pork pieces.
14. Raise the oil temperature to 355&deg;F
15. Fry each piece of pork again on each side for 30 seconds, removing any remaining bread crumbs between pieces. 
16. Drain the oil from each piece for a couple of minutes before serving.
